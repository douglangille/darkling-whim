# Scene Writing Prompt Engineering

*A complete progression from basic scene prompt to production-ready, publish-quality output optimization.*

## Evolution Summary

This document traces the iterative optimization of a fiction scene writing prompt through 11 progressive refinements, each adding layers of specificity, cinematic guidance, and output quality controls.

**Key Evolution:**
- Basic scene brief → Optimized → Production-ready → Hyper-directive → Ultra-compressed → Storyboarded → One-shot → Hyper-visual → Final publish-ready with sentence-level nudges

---

## Version 1: Basic Scene Brief

```markdown
## Prompt

Write the first draft of this scene at ~500 words. Just the text, no commentary.
Follow the beats and style guide closely.  
Make the setting feel alive and let Elara's inner tension show in small ways.

## Context

### The Rusted Lantern Tavern

A low-ceilinged seaside tavern in the port district of Greyharbor.
- **Smell:** Brine, woodsmoke, and overpoured ale.
- **Sounds:** Lute in the corner, gulls outside, low rumble of gossip.
- **Lighting:** Dim, mostly from the namesake lantern hung above the bar.
- **Patrons:** Sailors, dockworkers, and the occasional hooded stranger.
- **Notable Detail:** The lantern never goes out — rumor says it's filled with ghostlight.

### Elara Vance

- **Role:** Out-of-work mapmaker with a knack for finding trouble.
- **Appearance:** Short auburn hair, ink-stained fingers, green eyes.
- **Quirks:** Always humming half-remembered sea shanties.
- **Goal:** Recover a lost sea chart stolen by an unknown patron.
- **Conflict:** Haunted by debts owed to a dangerous maritime guild.

### Scene 1 Beats

1. Elara enters the tavern, scanning the room for her contact.
2. She notices the ghostlit lantern flicker — a bad omen.
3. The bartender slips her a folded note instead of speaking.
4. A drunken sailor blocks her exit as she tries to leave.
5. A sudden fight breaks out, forcing her to choose between escaping or helping.

### Style Guide

- **Tone:** Cinematic, slightly noir.
- **Pacing:** Fast transitions between beats; keep tension high.
- **Dialogue:** Snappy, laced with dry wit.
- **Description:** Sensory-rich; use setting details to reflect mood.
- **POV:** Close third-person, limited to Elara.
```

## Version 4: Hyper-Directive Cinematic

**Key Improvements:** Paragraph-level beat mapping for tight pacing control.

```markdown
## Hyper-Directive Cinematic Scene Draft Prompt

Write a first draft of ~500 words in **close third-person limited POV from Elara Vance**. No commentary—just narrative. Each paragraph should hit a specific micro-beat to maximize cinematic tension, sensory detail, and character psychology.

### Setting: The Rusted Lantern Tavern
- Low-ceilinged seaside tavern in Greyharbor's port district.  
- **Smells:** Brine, woodsmoke, ale.  
- **Sounds:** Lute, gulls, murmurs, occasional clatter.  
- **Lighting:** Ghostlit lantern above the bar; flickers foreboding.  
- **Patrons:** Sailors, dockworkers, hooded strangers.  
- Environment should feel lived-in; minor sensory details reflect mood and foreshadow danger.

### Character: Elara Vance
- Out-of-work mapmaker drawn into trouble.  
- **Appearance:** Short auburn hair, green eyes, ink-stained fingers.  
- **Quirks:** Hums fragments of sea shanties when nervous.  
- **Goal:** Recover a stolen sea chart.  
- **Conflict:** Owes debts to a maritime guild.  
- **Inner Life:** Reveal tension, calculation, and instinctive reactions via micro-behaviors, subtle thoughts, and fleeting gestures.

### Paragraph-Level Scene Beats
1. **Entrance:** Elara crosses the threshold; scan the room, notice patron types, gauge mood. Micro-gestures: adjusting cloak, ink-stained hands fidgeting, quick glances.  
2. **Ominous Lantern:** Lantern flickers; Elara's reaction is subtle—a tightened jaw, paused breath, momentary unease. Foreshadow danger.  
3. **Silent Note:** Bartender slides a folded note; show tension through Elara's hesitations, finger movements, eye focus, and rapid internal calculation.  
4. **Blocked Exit:** Drunken sailor impedes her; describe body positioning, spatial awareness, immediate threat perception, and mental options racing.  
5. **Fight Emergence:** A brawl erupts; split attention between environment and decision-making. Highlight physical instincts, sensory overload, and split-second judgments: escape or intervention.  

### Style and Guidance
- **Tone:** Cinematic noir, suspenseful, slightly gritty.  
- **Pacing:** Rapid, beat-driven paragraphs; alternate action, sensory immersion, and internal thought.  
- **Dialogue:** Snappy, dry-witted, economical. Avoid exposition.  
- **Description:** Sensory-rich (visual, auditory, tactile), reflective of Elara's state. Minor details should carry foreshadowing or tension cues.  
- **Micro-Tension:** Use gestures, facial tics, small habits, and environmental reactions to show stress and decision-making.  
- **Cinematic Techniques:** Vary sentence rhythm and paragraph length to control tension and pacing. Each paragraph should feel like a small, self-contained beat leading to the next.

Deliver a cohesive, suspenseful, cinematic narrative balancing environment, action, and Elara's internal tension.
```

## Key Learnings

### Progressive Optimization Principles

1. **Start with clear structure** - Beats, setting, character basics
2. **Add specificity** - Micro-gestures, sensory details, POV clarity
3. **Integrate cinematic language** - Shot types, framing, visual flow
4. **Build tension controls** - Paragraph-level beats, pacing guidance
5. **Compress for efficiency** - Single-paragraph director's instructions
6. **Add sentence-level guidance** - Maximize control without over-constraining

### What Makes a Publish-Ready Prompt

**Essential Elements:**
- Clear POV and voice constraints
- Paragraph-level beat structure
- Sensory immersion requirements
- Micro-behavior emphasis over emotion statements
- Cinematic framing language
- Tone and pacing controls
- Dialogue style specifications
- One-shot optimization for minimal revision

### When to Use Each Version

**Basic (V1-2):** Initial exploration, flexible outputs

**Optimized (V3-4):** Standard scene drafting with good quality

**Compressed (V5-6):** Rapid generation of multiple scenes

**One-Shot (V7-9):** Production work requiring minimal editing

**Final (V10-11):** Publish-ready output, maximum control

### Application to Different Genres

**Noir/Thriller:** Use full visual storyboarding, micro-tension emphasis

**Romance:** Focus on internal thought, fleeting reactions, sensory intimacy

**Fantasy:** Emphasize world-building details, magical system cues

**Literary:** Increase metaphor guidance, rhythmic sentence variation

**Action:** Maximize sensory overload, fragmented sentences, rapid POV shifts

## Usage Notes

- Adapt character/setting details for your specific scene

- For longer scenes, break into multiple prompts using numbered beats
- Test different models—some respond better to compressed vs. detailed prompts
- Save successful outputs to refine prompt templates over time
- Version control your prompts alongside your drafts
