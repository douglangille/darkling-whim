# ACT III CONTEXT: Digital Craftsmanship in the Age of Copilots

**Thesis:**  
Automation delivers precision, but not presence.  
Digital craftsmanship means ensuring the human heart of your work — attention, taste, intention — remains visible. The joy of making comes from the analogue part that no machine can imitate.

**Narrative arc**
1. **Opening:** The tactile scene — a hand adjusting a tool, light on metal, quiet focus.  
2. **Middle:**  
   - Collaboration as the highest form of craft; scheming sessions as handmade art.  
   - What disappears when energy is replaced by efficiency.  
   - Joy as the signal of authenticity.  
3. **Turn:** The craftsman’s new role: to leave evidence of humanity in the work.  
4. **Close:** The tool is set aside; conversation continues — creation as communion.

**Tone:**  
Intimate, elegiac, precise.  
Language shifts from technical to human; finish on warmth and stillness.

**Key motifs:** touch, grain, heartbeat, hands, shared laughter, silence after collaboration.

**Call to Action (CTA): The Human Signature Check**
Before publishing or presenting anything made with automation, ask:
1. Where is my judgment visible?  
2. What choice reflects care or restraint?  
3. Would another human recognize *me* here?  
If not, add a trace of yourself. That’s the signature of real craft.
