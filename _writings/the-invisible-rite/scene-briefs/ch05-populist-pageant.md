# Chapter 5 — The Populist Pageant
## Scene Briefs

**Purpose:** Introduce Cross as the showman-president; establish Anna's private unease and the Heritage Order's invisible orchestration.

---

## Scene 1 — The People's Hall Announcement (S)

**Location:** White House East Room  
**POV:** President Arlen Cross (limited 3rd)  
**Goal:** Deliver national address unveiling the ballroom project

**Obstacle:** Teleprompter failure; aides scrambling

**Conflict/Action:** Cross ad-libs phrases ghost-written by Voss—language heavy with ritual cadence

**Climax/Turn:** The crowd erupts in near-religious chant; Cross feels invincible

**Outcome:** Ratings soar, but the temperature in the East Wing literally rises a degree

**Tone/Mood:** Hot lights, brass band, gold bunting

**Symbol:** Populism as invocation; applause as spell

---

## Sequel 1 — Anna Cross Observes (Q)

**POV:** Anna Cross  
**Emotional Reaction:** Pride mixed with dread—her husband's eyes look glassy, his phrasing alien

**Reflection:** She recalls old campaign speeches that sounded human

**Decision:** Quietly order a private medical check; suspects exhaustion, not possession

**Exit Beat:** From her seat she notices chandeliers trembling though no one else does

**Mood:** Glamour undercut by vibration

---

## Scene 2 — Post-Speech Debrief (S)

**Location:** West Wing conference room  
**POV:** Cross (continued)  
**Goal:** Aides analyze the speech's viral success

**Obstacle:** Cross's manic insistence on repeating "the harmonic phrase"

**Conflict/Action:** Press secretary argues to drop the rhetoric; Cross overrules him

**Climax/Turn:** Televised replay triggers faint resonance in building microphones

**Outcome:** Technical anomaly logged, ignored

**Tone/Mood:** Champagne, feedback hiss

**Symbol:** Technology echoing ritual power

---

## Sequel 2 — Cross Alone (Q)

**Emotional Reaction:** Adrenaline high feels divine

**Reflection:** Hears a low tone when eyes close; assumes tinnitus

**Decision:** Approve overnight construction to "keep the energy moving"

**Exit Beat:** Exterior shot—cranes swing under moonlight, forming rough compass

**Mood:** Triumph shading toward mania

---

## Writing Notes

- Cross's voice: folksy bombast, but scripted phrases feel "off"
- Anna as emotional anchor—reader sees through her eyes what Cross can't
- Ritual language disguised as patriotic rhetoric
- Visual: gold, light, symmetry = beautiful lie
