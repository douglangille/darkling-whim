# Chapter 3 — Blueprints and Ghosts
## Scene Briefs

**Purpose:** Anchor Mercer's rationality, reveal the father link, and plant his first conscious decision to pursue the pattern.

---

## Scene 1 — The Archives at Night (S)

**Location:** National Archives  
**POV:** Cal Mercer  
**Goal:** Analyze the rubbing and the vault schematics in controlled conditions

**Obstacle:** Partial data—half the copper inscription corroded; colleagues unavailable

**Conflict/Action:** He overlays digital scans and discovers precise alignment with L'Enfant's survey grid

**Climax/Turn:** Notices a margin note in his father's handwriting referencing "node B-East Wing"

**Outcome:** Rational mind stumbles; personal history enters the case

**Tone/Mood:** Cold fluorescence; hum of air vents mimicking the vault tone

**Symbol:** The Archive as catacomb of unfinished thoughts

---

## Sequel 1 — Echo of the Father (Q)

**Emotional Reaction:** Conflicted pride and resentment; the old man's ghost feels near

**Reflection:** Wonders if the elder Mercer engineered part of the bunker network

**Decision:** Request internal records under pretext of "structural provenance"

**Exit Beat:** Leaves building at dawn—sunlight flashes off Capitol dome in perfect alignment with his path

**Mood:** Bleary wonder; geometry following him

---

## Scene 2 — Morning Briefing (S)

**Location:** White House liaison office  
**POV:** Cal Mercer  
**Goal:** Present preliminary findings to White House contact

**Obstacle:** Bureaucratic impatience, security classification

**Conflict/Action:** Dunne downplays anomalies; Mercer insists on further testing

**Climax/Turn:** Supervisor orders him to "hand it off." He refuses politely.

**Outcome:** Gains thin authority but paints target on himself

**Tone/Mood:** Fluorescent war-room light, coffee, clipped dialogue

**Symbol:** Reason negotiating with power

---

## Sequel 2 — Private Resolve (Q)

**Emotional Reaction:** Irritated by dismissal, energized by doubt

**Reflection:** Realizes he wants truth for himself, not the report

**Decision:** Visit his mentor Vale; she'll know how to read this

**Exit Beat:** Drives past the Mall; aerial geometry visible through windshield

**Mood:** Rational curiosity hardening into obsession

---

## Writing Notes

- Balance analytical sequences with emotional memory
- Father's presence should feel haunting but not supernatural
- Show Mercer's growing isolation from institutional colleagues
- Geometry becoming visible motif: patterns everywhere once noticed
