## Usage Guidelines

### For AI Assistants/Chatbots

When working with health-related queries:

1. **Review Current Context First**
   - Check `ozempic-therapy-tracking.md` for latest therapy status and baseline metrics
   - Reference active treatment documents before providing health advice
   - Note recent updates and medical appointment outcomes

2. **Maintain Continuity**
   - Update tracking documents with new information from conversations
   - Add observations to weekly tracking sections
   - Document side effects, symptom changes, and lifestyle adjustments
   - Record medical appointment outcomes and provider feedback

3. **Respect Medical Authority**
   - Always defer to Dr. Sharma and Dr. Massaro's guidance
   - Provide evidence-based information to support informed discussions with providers
   - Flag concerns that require medical attention
   - Never contradict explicit medical advice

4. **Track Interconnections**
   - Weight loss affects: BP, cholesterol, sleep apnea, testosterone, gout
   - Note medication interactions and dose adjustment needs
   - Monitor for cascade effects across multiple conditions

5. **Privacy & Safety**
   - This folder contains sensitive medical information
   - Maintain HIPAA-appropriate language and documentation standards
   - Track concerns systematically for provider discussions

### For Human Users (Doug)

1. **Regular Updates**
   - Add weekly observations to active tracking documents
   - Update metrics after medical appointments
   - Document medication changes, side effects, lifestyle shifts
   - Note questions or concerns for upcoming provider visits

2. **Before Medical Appointments**
   - Review tracking documents for trends and patterns
   - Prepare questions based on documented observations
   - Bring relevant data points to discussions

3. **After Medical Appointments**
   - Update documents with provider feedback and decisions
   - Document new prescriptions, dose changes, or treatment plans
   - Record next appointment dates and required prep (bloodwork, etc.)

4. **Emergency Reference**
   - Current medications and doses documented in tracking files
   - Baseline metrics available for emergency care providers
   - Medical team contact information maintained
