Metaphor

 

Techniques > Use of language > Metaphor

 

Metaphor is one of the most complex and powerful tools of language and hence gets a section all to itself.

  • Metaphor basics: Understanding metaphor.
    • Metaphor definition: The basics of what metaphors are.
    • Parts of a metaphor: The individual elements of metaphors.
    • How metaphors work: How metaphors work in practice.
  • Physical metaphors: Taken from the 'real world'.
    • Metaphors and things: Making concepts concrete.
    • Metaphors and space: The power of three dimensions.
    • Metaphors and time: Casting time into space.
    • Metaphors and energy: Different forms of energy, different metaphors.
  • Sensory metaphors: That directly address our five senses.
    • Metaphors and touch: Addressing what we feel.
    • Metaphors and sight: Addressing what we see.
    • Metaphors and sound: Addressing what we hear.
    • Metaphors, taste and smell: Addressing the gustatory senses.
  • Types of metaphor: Ways of classifying metaphors.
    • Absolute metaphor: Separated subject and vehicle.
    • Active metaphor: New and not established.
    • Complex metaphor: Multi-layered.
    • Compound metaphor: With many parts.
    • Concrete metaphor: Using physical comparisons.
    • Dead metaphor: Normal language, no longer recognized as metaphor.
    • Dormant metaphor: Weak connection between vehicle and subject.
    • Dying metaphor: Unfashionable clich?
    • Extended metaphor: One subject, many sub-elements.
    • Implicit metaphor: Incomplete description.
    • Metonymy: Using one item to represent another.
    • Mixed metaphor: Mismatched combination of metaphors.
    • Pataphor: Extreme form of metaphor.
    • Root metaphor: Unrealized basic driver.
    • Simple metaphor: Single meaning and linkage.
    • Submerged metaphor: Use a part as a metaphor for something else.
    • Synechdochic metaphor: Use a part as metaphor for the whole.
  • Speech parts and metaphor: Metaphor in grammar.
    • Adjective metaphors: Enhancing noun metaphors.
    • Adverb metaphors: Enhancing verbs.
    • Noun metaphors: Standard A is B metaphor.
    • Prepositions and metaphor: Using prepositions to extend metaphor.
    • Verb metaphors: Metaphors of action.
  • Using metaphor
    • Creativity and metaphor: Making the strange familiar and the familiar strange.
    • Everyday use of metaphor: Embedded in the language and culture.
    • Metaphor and politeness: Using euphemisms to avoid embarrassment.
    • Poetry and metaphor: Stimulating with word painting.
    • Therapeutic use of metaphor: Helping people explore and heal the inner world.

See also

Meaning, Three Levels of Interpretation, Simplicity Principle, The Psychology of Height

 

Gordon, D. (1978). Therapeutic Metaphors, Capitola, CA: Meta Publications

Lakoff, G. and Johnson, M. (1980). Metaphors we live by, Chicago: University of Chicago Press

Sommer, E. and Weiss, D. (1995). Metaphors Dictionary, International Thomson Publishing Company
Stight, T.G. (1979). Metaphor and Thought: Educational uses of Metaphor. Cambridge University Press
