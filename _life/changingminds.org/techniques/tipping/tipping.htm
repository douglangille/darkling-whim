Tipping

 

Techniques > Tipping

 

For those working in service industries where tipping is the norm, here is a collated set of information on how you can maximize the tips you receive.

  • How to Get a Bigger Tip: Many ways...
    • Be Clean: And fresh as a daisy.
    • Be Efficient: Proactive, quick, accurate, etc.
    • Be the Boss: Act as if you are the restaurant manager.
    • Boost the Bill: Of which you get a percentage.
    • Compliment Them: Say something nice.
    • Effortful Help: Go out of your way to help.
    • Elegant Exit: How to get them back to tip more.
    • Encourage Credit Cards: So they think less about cash.
    • Encourage Drinking: Drinks add a lot to the bill and make them less cautious.
    • Faster Music: Speed them up. Get more covers.
    • Get Down: Squat down to talk to them.
    • Get Introduced: So you are known.
    • Give Candy: Chocolates or mints (and the 'one-plus-one').
    • Happy Face: Draw happy face or sun on the check.
    • Happy Introduction: Be pleased to see them.
    • Invisible Reminders: Get the words 'give a tip' in.
    • Keep Them Hungry and Thirsty: So they order more.
    • Kids First: Put the children at ease.
    • Like Them: Be like them and like them.
    • Make Food Emotional: Describe in emotive words.
    • Memory Master: remember orders, dishes, special requests, names (inc regular customers),...
    • Number Rounding: Nudge things so they round up nicely.
    • Patriotic Message: Create a national bond.
    • Personal Connection: Find something you have in common.
    • Personal Recommendation: Recommend things from the menu.
    • Positive Forecast: Say the weather will be good.
    • Praise Them: Congratulate them on great choices.
    • Pre-emptive Thanks: Before getting paid.
    • Puzzle Them: Give them something to do.
    • Repeat the Order: Using their words.
    • Scoop the Door: Catch more good tippers.
    • Seeding: Show what 'others' have tipped.
    • Sell More: Get them to spend more money.
    • Sob Story: Hint at woes.
    • Split Bill: Help each person pay separately.
    • Your Name: Make yourself a person.
    • Tell Them: The percentages (if they're bad tippers).
    • Touch Them: Gently, on the arm.
    • Weather Forecast: Be positive about the weather.
  • Articles on Tipping
    • Ask, Don't Tell: Lower-status persuasion.
    • Dress for Success: Clean and tidy.
    • Finding the Right Waiting Job: The right one for you, that is.
    • Maintaining Dignity: Sometimes it's not worth it.
    • The Gender Difference in Waiting: Waiters, waitresses and tips.
    • The Importance of Mood: Most techniques work on mood.
    • Waiter's Views: What do waiters think?
    • What Jobs Get Tipped?: More than just waiting table.
  • Waiting Table: Basic rules.
    • Managing Mistakes: How to handle them gracefully.
    • Taking the Order: Connect with them and be nice.
    • Serving Wine: Do it like the pros.
    • Serving Food: A great opportunity to impress.
