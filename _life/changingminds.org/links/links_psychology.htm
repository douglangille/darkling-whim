Links to Psychology information sites

Click on the web () symbol on the left to jump to the website. 

If you have any additional suggestions, please let us know.

 

Star ratings
 Brilliant--Best in class
 Excellent--significant stuff
 Good--mostly useful 
 Ok--some bits useful 
 Yuk--minimum stuff
  What is Psychology -- a readable set of articles on a range of different psychology topics.
  Psychometrics newsletter -- lots of useful information on the detail of MBTI, etc. Especially useful for consultants.
  The ABC of Psychology -- a big glossary with brief descriptions of over 1200 psychological terms. Especially useful for students.
  PsyBlog - A solid blog on social psychology that has been running since 2004. Well-researched and well-written. Highly recommended.
  Splendid set of links on psychometrics, with particular focus on job evaluation (and job-finding).
  The Gestalt Archive - Lots of great papers from the gods of Gestalt Psychology.
  Simply Psychology - A comprehensive site dedicated to UK A-Levels (taken by 17-18 year-olds). Lots of good stuff for the rest of us too.
  Team technology - Includes useful Jungian questionnaires, for both the individual and team styles.
  The Lucifer Effect - Philip Zimbardo's 'web of the book' that looks at how 'good people turn evil' - or at least get drawn into evil acts.
  Authentic Happiness - Martin Seligman's site on Positive Psychology. Excellent free questionnaires (needs free registration, but worth it).
  BehaveNet - Huge set of links, definitions etc. for psychiatrists. Moderately useful for the rest of us.
  Narcissism Revisited - An excellent resource on narcissism, covering significant detail on many aspects of this condition that seems surprisingly common in our 'me' oriented world.
  In-Mind - A very readable social psychology magazine. New site and few articles at time of review (Jan06) but scope to become very useful.
  Nonverbal behavior--loads of papers, links, etc. on non-verbal (=body language) stuff. 
  Loads of social psychology information from Trinity U.
  Social psychology network. Loads of links to loads of site.
  Personality theories - clearly described theories, by theorist. Courtesy of Dr. C. George Boeree.
  David Keirsey's personality page. Includes well-proven MBTI-alike personality test (that used to be free) plus plenty of discussion and descriptions.
  'Psych Site'. Loads of links to all things psychological.
  'Ptypes.' Includes MBTI/Keirsey-like personality test, plus lots of discussions about it.
  The Personality Project. Much detail and links on academic theories of personality.
  Psybox -- Psychology dictionary -- lots of brief entries.
  'Current Research in Social Psychology.' Online, free-access journal with lots of very academic papers.
  Emotional Intelligence: An excellent and easy-to-read site, with straightforward descriptions. Lots of free details about EI and a pay-for test.
  A super site on Emotional Intelligence, with articles by Goleman and other luminaries of the EI field. Academic and rigorous in style. No adverts -- 100% content.
  'psychologie-sociale.org': For French speakers, here's a social psychology site that I believe to be rather good...
  Frank Pajares' excellent 'Information on Self-Efficacy' -- Academic collection on self-efficacy, including papers by Bandura, Pajares and others.
  A helpful set of pages on Social Psychology. Good for undergraduates or the curious. Loads of useful links, too.
  Social Influence journal -- solid academic journal from Psychology Press about the deeper aspects of changing minds. New in 2006.
  A Beginner's guide to abnormal psychology. Nice and simple descriptions of the main mental illnesses.
  'Antiprocess': An interesting and wonderfully-illustrated discussion about "preemptive recognition and marginalization of undesired information by the synergistic interplay of high-priority acquired mental defense mechanisms"
