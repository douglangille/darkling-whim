# Literary Critique: Mitzy and the Butterfly

**Critique Date**: December 22, 2025  
**Original Story**: `_posts/2015-08-21-mitzy-and-the-butterfly.md`

---

"Mitzy and the Butterfly" is a haunting flash fiction piece that masterfully subverts fairy tale conventions to deliver a devastating psychological punch. The story succeeds on multiple levels, though it also reveals some areas where the craft could be refined.

## Narrative Structure and Pacing

The circular narrative structure—opening and closing with the identical line "White magic. My little sister. Gone"—creates a tragic inevitability that elevates the piece beyond simple horror. This bookending technique forces readers to reinterpret the opening once they reach the conclusion, a sophisticated move for flash fiction. The pacing accelerates effectively as Mitzy approaches the climax, with shorter sentences and paragraphs building tension. However, the middle section occasionally meanders through the maze sequences, which could be tightened without losing atmospheric effect.

## Character Development

Mitzy emerges as a complex protagonist whose jealousy and displaced rage feel psychologically authentic. The subtle exposition—"She's not really my sister. She's a faker. We were happy before she came along"—reveals her resentment economically. Yet the story brilliantly maintains ambiguity about whether June is truly "Fake June" or whether this is Mitzy's delusion. Larry the butterfly functions as a manipulative tempter figure, his sarcastic voice ("Princess-Not," "Toots") simultaneously entertaining and sinister. The characterization succeeds through dialogue and internal monologue rather than exposition.

## Thematic Resonance

The story operates as a dark meditation on sibling rivalry, magical thinking in children, and the dangerous consequences of unchecked resentment. The maze serves as both literal setting and metaphor for Mitzy's confused emotional state. The ambiguity surrounding whether this is fantasy or psychological break creates unsettling possibilities—is this truly a magical realm, or a disturbed child's violent dissociation? This ambiguity strengthens rather than weakens the narrative.

## Technical Execution

The prose demonstrates strong command of sensory detail ("the humidity of the closed canopy," "gurgling hot breath") and maintains consistent voice. However, some stylistic choices feel inconsistent. The deliberate fragments in internal monologue work well, but occasional awkward constructions like "she mimed" feel overly explanatory. The archaic "smited" (rather than "smitten" or "slain") may be intentional archaism but reads as potentially mistaken. The description of Larry vanishing "his counsel no longer needed" feels abrupt and could use smoother integration.

## The Ending's Impact

The ending delivers genuine tragedy, as Mitzy's quest to destroy the Goblin reveals itself as fratricide. The repetition of the opening line gains devastating new meaning—the "white magic" wasn't protection but the moment of murder, the sister truly gone by Mitzy's own hand. This twist avoids feeling cheap because it's been structurally prepared throughout, even as the specifics remain concealed until the final beat.

## Overall Assessment

This piece demonstrates mature handling of dark psychological themes and sophisticated narrative technique within severe length constraints. With minor technical refinements, it would be publication-ready for top-tier flash fiction venues specializing in dark fantasy or psychological horror.

## Specific Technical Issues to Address

1. **"Smited"** - Archaic/potentially incorrect verb form (line: "The Goblin must be smited")
   - Consider: "slain," "killed," or "destroyed"

2. **"She mimed"** - Over-explanatory (line: "Fly up, she mimed")
   - Consider: Show the gesture more naturally or cut explanation

3. **Larry's vanishing** - Telling rather than showing (line: "Larry vanished, his counsel no longer needed")
   - Consider: More concrete description of disappearance

4. **Middle pacing** - Some meandering in maze navigation sequences
   - Consider: Tighten repetitive movement descriptions

5. **YAML typo** - "overlay_image: /assets/images/8b973b71609dfded_640.jog"
   - Should be: .jpg

## Strengths to Preserve

- Circular structure with transformed repetition
- Larry's distinctive, darkly comic voice
- Psychological complexity and ambiguity
- Economical characterization
- Sensory atmospheric detail
- Compression and pacing in opening/closing
- Effective use of internal monologue
- Thematic depth (jealousy, self-deception, projection)

## Revision Opportunities

1. **Tighten middle section** - Reduce maze navigation by 10-15% without losing atmosphere
2. **Polish technical issues** - Address the specific items listed above
3. **Smooth transitions** - Particularly Larry's disappearance and the final approach
4. **Clarify without explaining** - Find ways to make spatial relationships clearer without adding exposition
5. **Strengthen sensory details** - A few more specific textures/sounds in key moments

## Questions for Revision Planning

- Should the ambiguity about reality vs. delusion be maintained, clarified, or deepened?
- Are there opportunities to add texture without expanding word count?
- Should Larry's nature (real/imagined) be more clearly established or remain mysterious?
- Does the time pressure (one hour) feel urgent enough throughout?
- Are there any moments where showing could replace telling without expanding length?
