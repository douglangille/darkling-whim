## Summary & Synopsis

### Logline
A jealous girl pursues her younger sister through a magical maze with murderous intent, only to discover she has killed the very person she sought to save.

### Short Summary (100 words)
Mitzy awakens in an enchanted maze after her little sister June is taken by a Goblin. Guided by Larry, a sarcastic talking butterfly, she arms herself with an iron spear and navigates the labyrinth to rescue June. However, Mitzy's internal monologue reveals her resentment toward her "fake" sister. When she finally confronts what she believes is the Goblin, she thrusts her spear with murderous rage—only to discover she has killed June herself. The story opens and closes with the same haunting line, revealing the tragedy through circular structure.

### Detailed Synopsis
The story begins with Mitzy's realization: "White magic. My little sister. Gone." She finds herself in an overgrown boxwood maze, confused about whether this is reality or dream. A talking butterfly named Larry appears and points her to a sign warning "You have one hour. Don't touch the walls."

Mitzy remembers June is trapped somewhere in the maze with "the Goblin." Her internal thoughts reveal complex feelings—she refers to June as "not really my sister" and "a faker" whom the family was happy before. Larry, speaking with casual sarcasm, encourages Mitzy to kill the Goblin before it eats her.

Mitzy pulls an iron post from the ground, creating a makeshift spear. With Larry scouting ahead, they navigate the maze's dead ends. Mitzy's internal monologue wavers between determination to save June and resentment ("June-Not-June"). 

As they near the center, they hear breathing—gurgling, hot breath. Larry scouts from above and confirms the Goblin is around the corner. When Larry vanishes with his "counsel no longer needed," Mitzy prepares for battle. She works herself into a rage, closes her eyes, and thrusts the Iron Ivy spear around the corner.

The spear penetrates flesh. A voice says weakly: "Mitzy, sister, what have you done?" Mitzy opens her eyes to the horror of what she's done. The story ends by repeating the opening line, now transformed in meaning: "White magic. My little sister. Gone."

## Characters

### Mitzy (Protagonist)
- **Age**: Young girl (age unspecified, likely 8-12)
- **Goals**: 
  - Surface goal: Rescue June from the Goblin
  - Hidden goal: Eliminate June/resolve her jealousy
- **Motivations**: 
  - Displaced rage at losing parental attention
  - Jealousy of the "new" sister
  - Desire to return to pre-June happiness
- **Internal Conflict**: Love/duty vs. resentment/hatred
- **Arc**: No traditional arc—she achieves her unconscious desire with devastating consequences
- **Voice**: Child-like observations mixed with darker impulses
- **Key Traits**: Determined, violent, self-deceiving, vulnerable

### June (Sister/Victim)
- **Role**: The absent presence that drives the plot
- **Characterization**: Seen only through Mitzy's distorted lens
- **Key Mystery**: Is she truly "Fake June" or is this Mitzy's delusion?
- **Function**: Object of both rescue and murder; tragic victim
- **Final Moment**: Her weak voice asking "what have you done?" reveals innocence

### Larry (The Butterfly Guide)
- **Role**: Trickster/tempter figure
- **Personality**: Sarcastic, manipulative, knowing
- **Speech Patterns**: Casual insults ("Toots," "Princess-Not"), matter-of-fact cruelty
- **Function**: 
  - Narrative guide (moves plot forward)
  - Psychological projection (voice of Mitzy's dark impulses?)
  - Supernatural element (authenticates the fantasy world)
- **Ambiguity**: Is he real or Mitzy's fractured psyche?
- **Key Line**: "Does it matter? June or Fake June? You have to go back into the Maze."

### The Goblin (Antagonist/Red Herring)
- **Presence**: Mentioned repeatedly, never directly shown until the reveal
- **Function**: Externalization of Mitzy's violent impulses
- **Reality**: June herself, misidentified through Mitzy's rage and closed eyes
- **Symbolic**: The monster within; projection of guilt

## Opening & Ending Analysis

### Opening Effectiveness
**Highly Effective**
- **Hook**: Immediate mystery and emotional punch
- **Fragment**: "White magic. My little sister. Gone." (three short sentences = urgency)
- **Questions Raised**: What is white magic? What happened? Where is she?
- **Tone Set**: Fairy tale language with ominous undertone
- **Disorientation**: Mirrors Mitzy's confusion; reader shares experience

### Closing Resonance
**Devastating and Perfect**
- **Repetition**: Same line as opening, now loaded with new meaning
- **Transformation**: "Gone" now means dead by Mitzy's hand
- **No Escape**: Circular structure = inescapable fate
- **Emotional Payoff**: Horror, tragedy, and inevitability converge
- **Unanswered Questions**: Productive ambiguities remain

### Structural Bookending
- Opening establishes mystery; closing solves it horribly
- First reading: confusion; second reading: foreshadowing visible
- Creates re-reading value: story transforms with knowledge of ending

### Emotional Payoff
- **Tragedy**: Mitzy achieves unconscious goal with terrible cost
- **Horror**: The monster was her all along
- **Pathos**: June's innocent final words
- **Recognition**: Reader's growing dread confirmed
- **Catharsis**: Release of built tension into devastating clarity
