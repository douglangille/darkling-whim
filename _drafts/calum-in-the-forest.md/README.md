## Story Synopsis

A photographer documenting forest anomalies discovers that observation itself accelerates transformation. Through his camera viewfinder, Calum sees patterns invisible to the naked eye—displacement curves in moss, shadows at wrong angles, bodies integrated into the terrain. His compulsion to measure, document, and understand drives him deeper into the phenomenon until he becomes part of what he's been photographing. The camera isn't documentation; it's invitation.

## Design Intent

### Neurodivergent Coding (Author Intent)

Calum is coded as neurodivergent through behavioral markers embedded in voice and action:
- Compulsion to measure, document, complete patterns
- "I need to understand" as driving force stronger than survival
- Sensory processing through equipment (camera, notebook, GPS)
- External processing systems and routines (protractor, measurements)
- Fragment sentences showing perseveration when pattern-seeking overrides executive function
- Technical language as self-regulation mechanism

**Ethical Framework**: The forest exploits adaptive cognitive traits (pattern recognition, observational precision, completion drive). These are strengths in normal contexts—the horror comes from a predatory force weaponizing them, not from the traits themselves.

**Representation Approach**: 
- Experiential, not explanatory
- No diagnosis or labeling
- Shown through voice and behavior, never stated
- Reader recognition is bonus, not requirement
- Horror from exploitation, not from difference

## Lessons Learned

### On Minimal Revision
- Single surgical change can be sufficient
- "Less is more" applies to revision too
- Not every identified improvement needs implementation
- Author's instinct about "too on-the-nose" is critical filter
- Voice consistency matters more than clever additions

### On Neurodivergent Representation
- Subtle behavioral markers > explicit diagnosis
- Show through voice and action, never tell through commentary
- Reader recognition is bonus, not requirement
- Ethical horror: trait exploited ≠ flaw that dooms
- Trust the reader to understand intentional choices

### On Assessment Process
- Author context dramatically reframes analysis
- Understanding craft behind choices protects them during revision
- "What works" analysis should include *why* it works
- Design intent must inform revision decisions
