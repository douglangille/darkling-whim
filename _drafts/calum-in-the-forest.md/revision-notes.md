### Changes Made

#### Hilltop Scene - Voice Consistency

**Location**: Paragraph beginning "From the hilltop, the forest spreads forever."

**Original**: "and the light... God, the light pulses in patterns, sweeps and returns."

**Revised**: "and the light... the light pulses in patterns, sweeps and returns."

**Rationale**: 
- "God" was the only exclamatory moment in the story
- Calum's voice is technical, measured, precise throughout
- The ellipsis + repetition ("the light... the light") now carries the overwhelm
- Maintains voice consistency from first word to last
- Protects neurodivergent coding by keeping technical processing language intact

**Word Impact**: -5 words (1,100 → 1,095)

**Quality Check**: ✅ Voice now consistent throughout. Technical precision maintained. No new issues created.

### Lessons Learned

**On Neurodivergent Representation**:
- Subtle behavioral markers > explicit diagnosis
- Show through voice and action, never tell through commentary
- Reader recognition is bonus, not requirement
- Ethical horror: trait exploited ≠ flaw that dooms
- Trust the reader to understand intentional choices

**On Minimal Revision**:
- Single surgical change can be sufficient
- "Less is more" applies to revision too
- Not every identified improvement needs implementation
- Author's instinct about "too on-the-nose" is critical filter
- Voice consistency matters more than clever additions

**On Assessment Process**:
- Author context dramatically reframes analysis
- Initial assessment needed update after learning design intent
- "What works" analysis should include *why* it works
- Understanding the craft behind choices protects them during revision
