**Assistant:**

Simple version:  
- **Protest** = expression of disagreement.  
- **Riot** = protest that becomes violent or chaotic.  
- **Insurrection** = violent attempt to overthrow authority or government.  

---

### 1. **Protest**
- Purpose: express opposition or demand change.  
- Tactics: speeches, marches, sit-ins, petitions.  
- Legality: usually legal and protected (e.g., freedom of assembly).  
- Violence: typically **non-violent**—though emotion can run high.

**Example:**  
Students march to demand lower tuition fees.

---

### 2. **Riot**
- Purpose: often spontaneous, not strategic.  
- Tactics: destruction, looting, attacks—chaos replaces message.  
- Legality: illegal due to property damage and public danger.  
- Violence: **central feature**, not a by-product.

**Example:**  
After a sports loss, crowds smash windows and set fires.

---

### 3. **Insurrection**
- Purpose: seize power or overthrow legitimate authority.  
- Tactics: organized violence, armed uprising, or occupation of state institutions.  
- Legality: treasonous under most laws.  
- Violence: **instrumental**—directed at controlling or replacing government.

**Example:**  
Armed militants storm a national legislature to block a transfer of power.

---

### Quick Diagnostic
| Intent | Organization | Violence | Target |
|---------|---------------|-----------|---------|
| **Protest** | Planned | Rare/minimal | Policy or injustice |
| **Riot** | Spontaneous | High | Public order / property |
| **Insurrection** | Organized | Directed | Government authority |

So: all insurrections involve riots, some riots grow out of protests, but most protests never become either.
