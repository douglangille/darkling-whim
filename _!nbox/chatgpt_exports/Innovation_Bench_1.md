BLUF: The **Agile Manifesto** (2001) and the **Lean Manifesto** (1990s Lean Software / Lean Startup lineage) share DNA in valuing people, learning, and adaptability, but their emphases differ: **Agile** is about *how teams deliver software*, while **Lean** is about *removing waste and creating value through continuous learning and flow*.  

---

## **Agile Manifesto (2001)**
**Core Values**  
- **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools  
- **Working software** over comprehensive documentation  
- **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation  
- **Responding to change** over following a plan  

**Emphasis**  
- Born in software development ‚Üí tactical, team-level practices.  
- Focuses on short iterations, feedback loops, and flexibility.  
- Customer engagement and working product increments as proof of progress.  
- Culture of collaboration and adaptability.  

---

## **Lean Manifesto / Lean Principles**
**Core Principles** (adapted from Lean Manufacturing ‚Üí Lean Software Development)  
- **Eliminate waste** (activities not creating value)  
- **Amplify learning** (fast feedback, validated learning)  
- **Decide as late as possible** (defer irreversible decisions until evidence emerges)  
- **Deliver as fast as possible** (optimize flow, reduce cycle time)  
- **Empower the team** (push decision-making to those closest to the work)  
- **Build integrity in** (quality, resilience, technical excellence baked in)  
- **See the whole** (optimize the system, not just parts)  

**Emphasis**  
- Rooted in manufacturing ‚Üí systemic, value-stream perspective.  
- Focus on flow efficiency, waste reduction, and validated learning.  
- Broader than software: foundation for **Lean Startup** (build‚Äìmeasure‚Äìlearn).  

---

## **Comparison**
**Similarities**
- Both value **people over bureaucracy** (team empowerment, customer focus).  
- Both emphasize **learning and adaptation** (Agile through iteration, Lean through validated learning and delayed decisions).  
- Both reject rigid plans in favor of responsiveness.  
- Both became antidotes to heavyweight, waterfall-style project management.  

**Differences**
- **Agile = tactical / delivery focus**  
  - Short iterations, working software, customer collaboration.  
  - Team-level mindset and practices.  
- **Lean = systemic / strategic focus**  
  - Waste elimination, flow optimization, whole-system view.  
  - Organizational-level mindset, ties to economics and manufacturing.  
- **Agile metrics:** working features, team velocity, sprint reviews.  
- **Lean metrics:** cycle time, lead time, cost of delay, validated learning.  

---

## **Where They Intersect Today**
- **Scaled Agile Frameworks (SAFe, LeSS, Disciplined Agile)** often marry Agile practices with Lean thinking.  
- **Lean Startup** blends both: Agile sprints for product delivery + Lean feedback loops for validation.  
- Many organizations talk about ‚ÄúLean-Agile‚Äù to mean **fast learning, empowered teams, and value-stream optimization**.
