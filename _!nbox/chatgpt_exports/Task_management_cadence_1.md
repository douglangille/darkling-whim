**Assistant:**

You don’t have a task-management problem; you have a **cadence and trust** problem. The system you’re using works in the short term (calendar, reminders, external triggers) but fails for longer-horizon, non-urgent commitments because there’s no forcing function that makes you revisit them until they become a crisis.  

### Why the “guilt list” happens
- **Non-time-bound = no trigger.** If there’s no deadline, your brain doesn’t treat it as real until it’s urgent.  
- **Capture vs. cadence mismatch.** You’re capturing things reactively, but you don’t have a trusted rhythm for reviewing and deciding.  
- **Energy/context driven.** ADHD/autistic traits mean you’ll naturally bias toward in-the-moment, interest-driven work unless an externalized cadence provides scaffolding.  

### What actually works in your case
1. **Keep your “Now/Next/Later” buckets** — they fit your need for simplicity and low cognitive load. But you need a trigger to trust them.
2. **Introduce a Weekly Reset (15–20 min).**
   - Once a week (Sunday evening?), glance at “Later” and promote 1–2 items into “Next.”  
   - If nothing’s worth promoting, leave it — but at least you’ve seen it. This breaks the “wait until urgent” cycle.  
3. **Quarterly Intentions = anchor list.**
   - Instead of “projects” or “goals,” just keep a short list of 3–5 quarterly anchors: barn fix, X writing milestone, one house/yard thing.  
   - These feed “Later” — so when you do the weekly reset, they’re always visible.  
4. **Hobby/writing cadence hack.**
   - Don’t treat them like chores. Instead, block a recurring **“Studio Hour”** on calendar (weekly, biweekly).  
   - In that block, either advance writing or tinker with another creative project. Low-pressure, but cadence forces momentum.  

### Key shift
- **Don’t manage all tasks.** Trust your calendar and reactive energy for the bulk of life.  
- **Only manage the “slippery” stuff** (quarterly irregulars, long-term personal projects). For those, cadence is king: weekly reset + quarterly anchors + one creative block.  

That’s it. Three small levers. Everything else you’re already handling fine.
