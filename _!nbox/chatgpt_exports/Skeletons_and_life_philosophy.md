**Assistant:**

Absolutely — and that’s a sharp instinct. Camus belongs on that shelf.  

While the Stoics looked for alignment with *logos* (the rational order of the universe), Camus looked straight at the silence of the universe and said, “Fine. I’ll live anyway.” Both schools end up in the same behavioral neighborhood: composure, discipline, presence — but for different reasons.  

**Stoicism:** The cosmos has order; live well within it.  
**Absurdism:** The cosmos has no order; live well *despite* it.  

In practice, they converge. Seneca counsels *amor fati* — love your fate. Camus’ Sisyphus *chooses* his fate. Marcus seeks tranquility through reason; Camus through revolt.  

If you’re keeping a “canon of the living,” the triad works beautifully:  
- **Seneca** — serenity in discipline.  
- **Marcus** — perspective in impermanence.  
- **Camus** — defiance in futility.  

Together, they form a kind of existential stoicism — the philosophy of dancing skeletons.
