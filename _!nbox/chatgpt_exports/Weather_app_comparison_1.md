If you want a straight fight card:  

### Apple Weather (default)  
- **Pros**: Clean interface, built-in widgets, Siri/Spotlight integration, hyperlocal nowcast data (uses multiple providers).  
- **Cons**: Forecast detail is limited, reliability varies regionally (Atlantic Canada often mediocre).  

### Yr.no (Norwegian Meteorological Institute)  
- **Pros**: Strong global reputation for accuracy, especially for coastal/Atlantic weather. Very data-dense, excellent long-range models.  
- **Cons**: UI is clunkier, not as smooth in iOS ecosystem, lacks widget polish.  

### “Something else” (e.g., Carrot, AccuWeather, RadarScope)  
- **Carrot**: Fun personality, lots of sources, but a bit gimmicky unless you like the snark.  
- **AccuWeather/Weather Network**: Decent for North America, but advertising-heavy and sometimes laggy.  
- **RadarScope**: Best for weather nerds—raw radar, storm tracking. Overkill for casual daily use.  

### No Weather App at All  
- **Pros**: One less distraction. Just look outside, check Environment Canada alerts if needed.  
- **Cons**: You’ll be blindsided by rain, and NS weather changes fast enough to punish that choice.  

### Verdict for Milford, NS life  
- **Best all-rounder**: Yr.no (accuracy for coastal weather).  
- **Convenience fallback**: Apple Weather (widgets, quick glance).  
- **Power user pairing**: Yr.no + Apple Weather = accuracy + integration.  
- **Skip**: AccuWeather/Weather Network unless you enjoy push alerts and ads.

###  Accuracy Face-off: Nova Scotia Realities

- **Yr.no**

  - Built by a national meteorological agency with a strong track record and fine-grained modeling—even outside Europe.

- **Apple Weather**  
  - Bundles forecasts from a mix of sources (e.g. NOAA, The Weather Channel).  
  - General benchmarking (via ForecastWatch) shows it performs respectably in short-term nowcasts and 7-hour forecasts—but data is UK-based, not specific to Nova Scotiaciteturn0search2.
  - Heavily integrated into iOS, which is a plus—unless regional accuracy lags.
